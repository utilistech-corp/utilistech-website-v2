What a Microsoft 365 AI integration consultant actually does

Most "AI consultants" chasing UK SMEs are generalists who pitch Copilot as a drop-in button. In practice, a Copilot rollout that delivers real hours-saved depends on three things that get skipped:

  • Data hygiene — Copilot grounds on whatever your users can already see. Sprawling SharePoint permissions leak data into prompts.
  • Sensitivity labelling — Without Purview labels, Copilot happily surfaces payroll spreadsheets to the sales team.
  • Use-case specificity — Generic prompts get generic value. Role-specific prompt libraries are where ROI shows up.

Utilis Technologies (UtilisTech) works with UK SMEs (typically 20–250 staff) across the full Copilot journey: readiness audit, governance setup, pilot, rollout, and the bespoke AI tools that live alongside Copilot where Copilot alone won't cut it.

Post-15 April 2026: what's changed

From 15 April 2026, Microsoft restricted Copilot Chat inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint and OneNote for users on the free Copilot Chat tier. Full in-app Copilot now requires a paid Microsoft 365 Copilot (Premium) licence — currently around £24.70 per user per month in the UK. For SMEs whose AI strategy was "everyone gets Copilot Chat for free and we'll figure it out from there", that strategy stopped working this month.

There are two shifts worth pricing into any 2026 M365 plan:

  • Per-task model choice. Copilot Premium now lets you choose between OpenAI models and Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.6 on a per-task basis — not a tenant-wide switch. The choice changes the tone and reasoning quality of outputs, but not what Copilot can do; that's still bounded by the Microsoft Graph and the apps Copilot sits inside.
  • Researcher agent outputs. Copilot's Researcher agent now exports findings as PowerPoint decks, PDFs, infographics or audio briefings — not just text. Useful if you're presenting research upwards; worth factoring into any Copilot business case.

The honest question after 15 April is: are you getting value from Copilot, or did you think you were because it lit up in Office? If it's the latter, a targeted custom automation usually beats scaling Premium licences to the whole team.

Copilot readiness checklist

Before you buy Copilot licences, walk through this. If you can tick 7+ of 10, you're ready. If you can't, a readiness project will pay for itself.

  • Licensing: you're on Microsoft 365 Business Standard/Premium or E3/E5, and you've identified who actually needs a paid Microsoft 365 Copilot (Premium) licence at ~£24.70 per user per month (post-15 April 2026, the free Copilot Chat tier no longer covers Word, Excel, PowerPoint or OneNote)
  • Entra ID: all users authenticate through Entra ID with MFA enforced via Conditional Access
  • SharePoint permissions: you've run a recent "oversharing" report and closed the obvious holes
  • Sensitivity labels: at least three Purview sensitivity labels are deployed and applied by default
  • Data Loss Prevention: baseline DLP policies exist for UK GDPR personal data
  • Teams governance: Teams creation policy is defined (not a free-for-all)
  • User readiness: target users already live in Outlook, Word, Excel and Teams daily
  • Hours-saved hypothesis: you can name the three workflows you expect Copilot to compress
  • Pilot group identified: 5–15 users across at least two functions
  • Acceptable Use Policy: you have — or are ready to adopt — a written AI usage policy for staff
