What is Microsoft 365 Copilot?

Microsoft 365 Copilot is an integrated AI assistant that works across Microsoft 365 applications including Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams. Powered by large language models and your organization's data and context, Copilot assists users with writing, analysis, research, and collaboration tasks.

Recent Update (16 April 2026): Microsoft has implemented new restrictions on Copilot Chat functionality and adjusted licensing models. Premium features now require approximately £24.70 per user per month. Users can now select Claude Opus 4.6 for specific per-task use cases, and Researcher agent outputs have been refined for enhanced accuracy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What data does Copilot have access to? ▼
Copilot can access data within your Microsoft 365 tenant that the logged-in user has permission to access. This includes emails, documents, chat messages, and collaborative content. Your data stays within your tenant and is not used to train Microsoft's models. We help configure sensitivity labels to control what Copilot can see.
Is Copilot compliant with UK data protection laws? ▼
Microsoft 365 Copilot is designed to comply with GDPR and UK data protection requirements when properly configured. Your data remains within your tenant, and you maintain control over access and retention policies. We audit configurations to ensure compliance with your specific regulatory obligations.
What are the licensing costs for Copilot? ▼
Standard Microsoft 365 licenses include basic Copilot functionality. Premium Copilot Chat features are available at approximately £24.70 per user per month. Enterprise agreements may offer different pricing structures. We help you understand licensing options and calculate total cost of ownership for your organization.
Can we use different AI models with Copilot? ▼
Microsoft 365 Copilot uses multiple large language models. Users can now select Claude Opus 4.6 for specific per-task use cases alongside Copilot's default models. This flexibility allows organizations to choose the best model for each business scenario while maintaining governance and security.
How do we measure Copilot ROI? ▼
We establish measurement frameworks focusing on time saved, quality improvements, and user satisfaction. Common metrics include: reduction in time spent on routine tasks, improved document quality, increased collaboration, and user adoption rates. We integrate these metrics into your existing business intelligence systems.
What happens if our data contains sensitive information? ▼
Sensitivity labelling and data classification are core components of our implementation approach. We help classify content by sensitivity level and configure Copilot policies to prevent it from accessing or processing highly sensitive data. This ensures Copilot enhances productivity while maintaining security.
How long does a typical implementation take? ▼
Timeline varies based on organizational size and complexity. A typical implementation includes: 2-4 weeks for readiness assessment, 4-6 weeks for governance setup and pilot, and 6-8 weeks for phased rollout. We develop a customized timeline during the initial consulting engagement.
