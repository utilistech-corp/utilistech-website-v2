Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly does a Microsoft 365 Copilot consultant do?

A consultant helps organizations successfully implement, deploy, and optimize Microsoft 365 Copilot. This includes assessing your current infrastructure, planning deployment strategy, training users, and ensuring the technology delivers measurable business value.

Is Copilot Pro worth it?

For individuals, Copilot Pro ($20/month) offers advanced features. For organizations, Microsoft 365 Copilot Pro licenses ($30/month per user) provide enterprise security, data handling, and admin controls. ROI depends on your use case and organization size.

What changed on April 15, 2026?

April 15, 2026 marked significant updates to Microsoft 365 Copilot licensing and capabilities. Pricing adjusted for enterprise customers, new models became available, and additional integration points were released. Check with your Microsoft account manager for specifics.

Should we use OpenAI's model or Claude?

Microsoft 365 Copilot uses Copilot Pro (powered by OpenAI's GPT-4) by default. Claude integration is available through custom plugins. The choice depends on your data sensitivity, compliance requirements, and specific use cases.

How long does deployment take?

Timeline varies by organization size and complexity. Small teams: 2-4 weeks. Mid-size: 4-8 weeks. Enterprise: 8-16 weeks. Planning, pilot testing, and full rollout stages all impact the timeline.

What's the difference between Copilot and custom AI tools?

Copilot is pre-built, integrated into Microsoft 365, and ready to use immediately. Custom tools offer tailored solutions but require more development time and ongoing maintenance. Often, the best approach combines both.

Do we need Microsoft 365 Enterprise to use Copilot?

Microsoft 365 Copilot requires a subscription to the appropriate Copilot Pro license tier. Your existing Microsoft 365 plan (Business Standard, Enterprise, etc.) determines what Copilot features are available.
