Is your business ready for AI? A practical checklist

Published March 15, 2026 | Updated April 12, 2026 | By Utilistech

AI adoption is no longer a question of if, but when. Yet many organizations rush into AI initiatives without assessing their true readiness. This checklist helps you evaluate your business's foundational readiness across five critical dimensions.

1. Infrastructure Readiness

  • Cloud infrastructure in place (AWS, Azure, or GCP)
  • Data storage systems (data lakes, data warehouses)
  • API integration capability across systems
  • Security frameworks compliant with AI requirements
  • Network bandwidth sufficient for AI workloads

2. Data Readiness

  • Data sources identified and documented
  • Data governance policies in place
  • Data quality baseline established
  • Historical data retention (12+ months minimum)
  • Data privacy and compliance frameworks (GDPR, CCPA)

3. Skills and Talent

  • Data science or ML engineering capability available
  • Data analysts on staff or available for hire
  • Business process expertise (domain knowledge)
  • Change management resources identified
  • Training budget allocated for upskilling

4. Business Process Maturity

  • Clear ROI targets defined for AI initiatives
  • Standardized processes to optimize
  • Metrics and KPIs in place
  • Organizational alignment on AI strategy
  • Budget approved for pilot and full deployment

5. Governance and Risk Management

  • AI ethics framework established
  • Model governance and monitoring plan
  • Bias testing and mitigation strategy
  • Compliance risk assessment completed
  • Stakeholder communication plan in place

Scoring Your Readiness

Score Readiness Level Recommendation
20-25 checks Highly Ready Proceed with strategic AI initiatives. Focus on scaling and optimization.
15-19 checks Moderately Ready Start with pilot programs. Address gaps in parallel with pilots.
10-14 checks Preparing Begin foundational work: build skills, establish governance, prepare data.
Below 10 Early Stage Focus on quick wins with low-risk AI tools to build internal capability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do we need all 25 items before starting AI?

No. Start with pilots using low-risk, well-defined use cases. Address readiness gaps in parallel as you learn.

What's the fastest way to improve readiness?

Prioritize: (1) data governance, (2) identifying use cases with existing data, (3) building internal expertise through training or hire.

What if we're lacking in skills?

Hire consultants for pilots, invest in training, partner with vendors who provide managed services. Upskilling takes time—plan accordingly.

How do we avoid ethical and compliance issues?

Start by defining your AI ethics principles, establish model governance early, and audit models for bias before deployment.
